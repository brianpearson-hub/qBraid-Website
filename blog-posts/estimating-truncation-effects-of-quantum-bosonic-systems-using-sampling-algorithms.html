Estimating truncation effects of quantum bosonic systems using sampling algorithms

20251 min read

Making resource estimation practical

Learn how classical sampling methods, such as Markov Chain Monte Carlo, can estimate truncation errors in simulating bosons on quantum computers, aiding resource assessment and result validation for quantum simulations, including applications in two-dimensional lattice scalar field theory.

Why sampling helps

  • Adaptive truncation keeps simulation size manageable while bounding errors
  • Sampling workflows integrate with qBraid SDK so researchers can plug in their models
  • Result validation increases confidence for expensive quantum runs

These techniques help teams plan experiments and interpret output from qBraid Lab and other devices.
